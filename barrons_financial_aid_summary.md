## Key Observations

**Enrollment Distribution:**
- No category institutions serve 6.4 million undergraduates (47% of total)
- C tier schools serve 2.9 million (21%)
- Most competitive schools serve 518k (4%)

**Progressive Pricing:**
- Most competitive schools: $8,865 for low-income vs $49,113 for high-income (5.5x difference)
- Middle tiers (C/C+): $15-16k for low-income vs $24k for high-income (1.5-1.6x difference)
- No category: $13,965 for low-income vs $19,174 for high-income (1.4x difference)

**Access and Debt:**
- Pell rates increase from 15.6% (Most competitive) to 44.5% (No category)
- Federal loan rates peak at C+/C tiers (53%), suggesting 'middle squeeze'
- Most competitive schools have lowest loan rate (21.4%) despite higher sticker prices

**Affordability Patterns:**
- For low-income students ($0-30k), most affordable: NC ($11,436), No category ($13,965)
- For high-income students ($110k+), costs range from $18,996 (NC) to $49,113 (Most competitive)
- Specialized programs are expensive across all income levels ($27,585 - $38,839)

## Methodology

**Data Sources:**
- Barron's Profiles of American Colleges 2013
- U.S. Department of Education College Scorecard (most recent cohorts)
- 6,149 undergraduate institutions (excluded 280 graduate-only programs)

**Metrics:**
- All values are medians across institutions in each category
- Pell Rate: % of undergraduates receiving Pell grants
- Loan Rate: % of undergraduates taking federal student loans
- Net Price: Cost of attendance minus all grant aid (federal, state, institutional)
- Income brackets based on NPT variables: npt41 ($0-30k), npt42 ($30-48k), npt43 ($48-75k), npt44 ($75-110k), npt45 ($110k+)
- Total Undergrads: Sum of undergraduate enrollment across all institutions in category
